Introduction

This document attempts to examine the impact of climate change on coastal areas in the northeastern United States. The amount of CO2 in the atmosphere has a direct impact on oceanic warming and hurricane formation. In 2012, Hurricane Sandy made landfall as a powerful tropical storm, severely damaging the Jersey Shore. Homes were destroyed by high winds, water, and storm surges.

The storm stretched from the Atlantic Ocean to the Great Lakes, hitting areas that would normally be safe from hurricane impacts.

  • $30 to $50 billion dollars in damages
  • Damage to both public and private property including coastline.

Literature Review

A literature review shows the history of hurricane and tropical storm categories that have made landfall in the northeast. So-called "storms of the century" are becoming more common and increasingly destructive.     Analysis

    Generally, the strength and impacts of these hurricanes has increased with the amount of CO2 present in the atmosphere. Hurricanes and their various impacts can be tracked by the following metrics:

    • Storm surge
    • Windspeed
    • Floodwater levels
    • Economic impacts

    From our research, all of the aforementioned indicators were much higher given the strengths of the storms. The economic loss associated with Hurricane Sandy is between $30 to $50 billion. The large range of this estimate highlights weaknesses in the data collection process.
